19 March 2025 / LOREAN

The ocean is a vast and mysterious part of our planet, covering more than 70% of Earth's surface. It plays a crucial role in regulating the climate, supporting marine life, and providing resources like food, oxygen, and even medicines. Beneath its surface lies a world teeming with biodiversity, from tiny plankton to the largest animals on Earth—the blue whales. The ocean also connects people across continents, shaping cultures, economies, and histories. Despite its importance, it faces serious threats from pollution, overfishing, and climate change, making ocean conservation more important than ever.

19 March 2025 / LILLY

Animals are an essential part of life on Earth, contributing to the balance of ecosystems and the diversity of our planet. They come in all shapes and sizes, from tiny insects to massive elephants, and inhabit every corner of the world—from deep oceans to high mountains. Animals play vital roles in nature, such as pollinating plants, dispersing seeds, controlling pests, and serving as food for other species. Many animals also share close bonds with humans, providing companionship, labor, and inspiration. However, habitat loss, climate change, and human activities threaten many species, highlighting the urgent need for wildlife protection and environmental care.

19 March 2025 / CAMMI

Food is a basic necessity of life, providing the energy and nutrients our bodies need to grow, function, and stay healthy. It also holds deep cultural, social, and emotional significance, often bringing people together through shared meals and traditions. Different regions around the world have unique cuisines that reflect their history, environment, and way of life. From simple home-cooked meals to elaborate dishes, food is a form of expression and identity. As the global population grows, issues like food security, sustainability, and nutrition have become more important, encouraging efforts to reduce waste and promote healthy, environmentally friendly eating habits.
